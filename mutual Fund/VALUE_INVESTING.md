If your goal is **a mutual fund that buys stocks at low price and sells them high**, then you are basically looking for funds that follow **value investing** or **dynamically adjust** based on market levels.

Here are the types of mutual funds that do this *by design*:

---

## ‚úÖ **1. Value Funds (Best match for ‚Äúbuy low, sell high‚Äù)**

These funds **search for undervalued stocks** and buy them when prices are low.

### ‚úî How they work

* Buy stocks that are fundamentally strong but temporarily down.
* Sell when the stock price goes up to fair value.
* Follow Warren Buffett‚Äìstyle investing.

### ‚úî Examples (India)

* **ICICI Prudential Value Discovery Fund**
* **Kotak India EQ Contra Fund**
* **Nippon India Value Fund**
* **HDFC Capital Builder Value Fund**

---

## ‚úÖ **2. Contra Funds**

Contra funds invest *against the market trend* ‚Äî they buy companies that are out of favour (low price).

### ‚úî Examples

* **Kotak India EQ Contra Fund**
* **SBI Contra Fund**

---

## ‚úÖ **3. Dynamic Asset Allocation / Balanced Advantage Funds**

These funds increase equity when market is **low** and reduce equity when market is **high**.

### ‚úî How they work

* When market crashes ‚Üí **Buy more equity**
* When market is expensive ‚Üí **Sell equity**, shift to debt
* Gives smoother returns over time

### ‚úî Examples (India)

* **ICICI Prudential Balanced Advantage Fund**
* **HDFC Balanced Advantage Fund**
* **Nippon India Balanced Advantage Fund**
* **Aditya Birla Sun Life Balanced Advantage Fund**

These are ideal if you want a fund that **automatically buys low and sells high** without your involvement.

---

## ‚úÖ **4. Multi-Asset Funds**

These move money between:

* Equity
* Gold
* Debt

They naturally rebalance, buying the asset which is low and selling the asset which is high.
