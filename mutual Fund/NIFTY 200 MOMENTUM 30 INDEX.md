## What is the Nifty 200 Momentum 30 Index

   * There is a cap on individual stock weight: capped at the **lower** of **5%** or **5√ó its free-float-only weight**.

3. **Rebalancing / Reconstitution**

   * In addition to scheduled review, there could be **ad-hoc reconstitution** if a stock is removed from Nifty 200

## Pros & Risks of This Momentum Strategy

**Pros:**

* **Potential for high returns**: Since momentum stocks are already on a strong upward trend, the index aims 
to capture these trending winners. ([mint][3])
* **Dynamic allocation**: Because weight depends not just on size, but on momentum score, it can tilt more 
toward fast-moving companies. ([Nifty Indices][2])
* **Systematic and transparent**: The methodology is rule-based (normalized returns + volatility), reducing

**Risks:**

* **Sensitivity to market cycles**: In down or sideways markets, momentum strategy may underperform. ([mint][3])

## ‚úÖ Things to Think About Before Investing in Momentum Funds

1. **Higher Volatility**
   Momentum strategies can be more volatile ‚Äî when trends reverse, these funds can suffer.

2. **Time Horizon**
   These may work well as a **satellite allocation** in your portfolio (not necessarily the core) ‚Äî especially 
   if your time horizon is medium to long.

3. **Expense Ratio & Turnover**
   For index-based momentum funds, expense ratio is important. For active momentum funds, turnover can be high 
   (because stocks are frequently replaced), which may have cost implications.

4. **Tax Implications**
   Since these might involve more frequent trading, consider how capital gains (short-term vs long-term) will 
   affect your after-tax returns.

5. **Allocation Size**
   Given the risk, many investors allocate only a **small portion** (say 5‚Äì15%) of their overall equities 
   portfolio to momentum funds.
